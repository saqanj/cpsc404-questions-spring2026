## Question
Corporate-sponsored contributors: who do they “owe” loyalty to?
HYBHY makes loyalty to your direct manager central, because managers reward loyalty and chain-of-command violations are punished. ProducingOSS says open source legitimacy comes from community trust and contribution, and forkability limits centralized power. So if you’re paid by a company to work on an OSS project, what happens when your manager wants one thing (roadmap, deadlines, PR wins) but the community consensus wants another? Is the corporate chain of command basically “imported” into the OSS space, or does the OSS governance structure force the company to play by different rules?
