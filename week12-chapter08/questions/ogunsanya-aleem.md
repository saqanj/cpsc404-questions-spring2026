## Question
In Help Your Boss Help You, Kousen argues that maintaining professional distance helps preserve clarity and effectiveness in communication, especially when delivering difficult or critical feedback. At the same time, being personally close to a boss might make conversations feel more comfortable on the surface.
Are employees more or less likely to communicate honestly with a boss they are personally close to? Does personal closeness encourage openness, or does it create pressure to avoid conflict, protect the relationship, or soften criticism in ways that reduce the quality of decision-making?
